„Drei Oberton-Klaviere (6chan)“

Year: 2021-2024

In "Drei Oberton-Klaviere," comprising three movements, the composer employs three different compositional techniques to work with the material of sampled piano sounds.

The first movement, „Glissandi," works with electroacoustic aesthetics moving through the Overton series, zooming in and out of the Sound of the Piano. Utilizing sampled sound material from three different pianos (Steinway, Yamaha, Fazioli), ranging from pianissimo to fortissimo, the composition moves fluidly through the whole range of frequencies presented in a piano.

In the minimalist approach of the second movement, "27,5*25=909," a single sounds are repeated, drawing the listener into a trance-like state. This movement questions our way of listening drawing our attention to the exact same sound, but hearing something new. We get closer and closer to the Timbre of the pianos forgetting its melodic use.

The final movement, "Fraktale," forms groups of notes in multiple ways using stochastic methods. Those groups start somewhere and end up somewhere else, the new space reminds us strongly of the initial one, just to move again somewhere new and to end up somewhere familiar.

„Is this Cairo? (9chan)“

Year: 2023

Is a composition that deals with Davids overwhelming experience during his sound research in Cairo. Funded by the „Die Neue Liszt Stiftung“ David stayed in Cairo for 15 days doing field-recordings, talking to sound researchers and musicologists and recording a traditional Sufi Drummer. In the composition you hear two recordings: One mono field recording that is placed on every of the 8 speaker around you and a drum loop (that is placed on one speaker) that was derived from one of the recording sessions with Mohamed Abouzid Hussein. The only compositional method is Volume automatons, that give glimpses into, for David very unfamiliar, sonic world. Inviting to explore and analyse these windows of sound.

„Die Kernkraft-Variation (8chan)“

Year: 2021

This composition was created after a one-month working stay at the Swiss nuclear power plant Leibstadt (KKL), where I had the opportunity to record places that are inaccessible to most people. From these recordings emerged 'The Nuclear Power Variation', in which I engaged with the themes of form, spectrum, and energy. Virtually all the sounds heard originated from recordings made on-site, which were shaped through editing or left in their original state to produce the desired sonic quality.
